# Andalucía Real-Estate Scouting Trip — 5-Day Master Plan
**Dates:** Wednesday 12 Aug → Saturday 15 Aug 2026 (4 nights, drive back Sat night)  
**Party:** 5 people (incl. 1 young child) + 7-seater Mercedes V-Class (Android Auto)  
**Motive:** Evaluate Obra Nueva + Suelo Urbano (terrenos) + Resale in suburban/commuter belts  
**Strategy:** Three working days of scouting + two travel days. Padel on the **final scouting morning** (Sat 15) before the drive back to Madrid.

## 1. GEOGRAPHIC STRATEGY — why these two clusters

You said: "suburbs of suburbs, outer commuter rings, quiet residential towns, more space, better value, no city centres." That rules out Málaga capital, Marbella city, Sevilla capital, Granada capital, Estepona centro, Fuengirola centro. Two clusters best match the three pathways:

| Cluster | Best for Obra Nueva | Best for Suelo Urbano (terrenos) | Best for Resale | Schools | Padel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Inland Guadalhorce** (Alhaurín de la Torre, Cártama, Coín, Alhaurín el Grande) | Limited but cheaper small builder infill | **Best in region** (€80–€450/m² urban plots) | **Best €/m²** for 3-bed family villas (€180k–€450k) | El Pinar (verified), Novaschool Sunland | Municipal courts; smaller clubs |
| **Costa del Sol central** (Mijas Costa / La Cala / Las Lagunas / Riviera del Sol) | **Strong** (Taylor Wimpey La Cala Golf, Metrovacesa Almare, multiple Kronos / Aedas projects) | Mid (€250–€600/m²) | Mid (€280k–€650k for 3-bed) | St Anthony's College (verified), The Ark | Raquetas de Mijas, Oxygen Padel |
| **Costa del Sol western** (Cancelada, El Paraíso, Atalaya, Benahavís) | **Strongest** (Haiku, Adagio, Estepona Hills, Omnia, Atalaya Emotion, Fuente Lirios) | Limited and expensive (€4,500+/m² buildable villa plots in Benahavís) | High (€400k–€800k for 3-bed) | The International School Estepona, Atalaya International | **NAC Padel** (world's best) + Estepona Tennis & Padel Club |

**Why 2 bases, not 1:**  
- Inland cluster ↔ coast cluster = 25–35 min via the A-7053 or A-357. Trying to do both from a single base adds 50+ min/day of dead driving.  
- Western Costa ↔ central Costa = 30–40 min via A-7. Same logic.  
- Net: 2 base changes × 1 move per day = 2 hours of total relocation cost, saving ~6 hours of return-trip driving and giving you 2 fresh "morning-of" school/pre-school searches.

**Why this layout works for 5 calendar days / 4 nights:**
- The first day is dominated by the 5h Madrid→Málaga drive; you only have ~5 hours of useful scouting on Day 1 (after settling into the villa).
- The middle days (Thu 13 + Fri 14) are the **only** two full working days of real estate.

- This packs ~3.5 working days of scouting into a 4-night itinerary with a single padel session — exactly what you asked for.

## 4. DAY 2 — Thursday 13 August: Inland Loop — Plots & Resale

This is the **Suelo Urbano (terrenos) deep-dive day** — inland Andalucía is where the value-per-m² is highest for buildable urban land. Also a great day to look at the cheaper resale stock (€180k–€400k for 3-bed).

### Morning — Cártama + Estación de Cártama (~40 min from base)
- **09:00** Depart base.
- **09:30** Arrive **Estación de Cártama** (the train-station suburb of Cártama, where the action is).
  - This is a fast-growing commuter suburb. Cheap townhouses, small obra nueva, growing supply.
  - Drive the urbanizaciones behind the train station (Barriada de la Estación, Nueva Aljaima, El Sexmo). Look for "se vende" signs.
  - **Reference address for new build:** Novaschool Sunland International, Ctra. Cártama-Pizarra, Nueva Aljaima, 29580 Cártama Estación (this school is the magnet for expat families — being within walking distance is a resale-value boost).
    - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Novaschool+Sunland+International+Cartama+Estacion
- **10:30** **Mercadona Cártama** (C/ Alemania s/n, Cártama) — stock up, morning coffee stop. Big parking, child-friendly.
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mercadona+Calle+Alemania+Cartama
- **11:00** **Lidl Cártama** (Ctra. MA-421, s/n, Cártama, on the Pizarra road) — 1.3 km from Cártama pueblo. Verify address on the day.
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Lidl+Carretera+MA-421+Cartama

### Late morning — Coín (~25 min from Cártama)
- **11:30** Drive into Coín pueblo via A-355. **Drive the main road Av. Reina Sofía** — Mercadona + Lidl are side by side, the retail anchor.
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mercadona+Avenida+Reina+Sofia+Coin
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Lidl+Avenida+Reina+Sofia+50+Coin
- **12:00** Coín is the cheapest town in the corridor for **3-bed resale villas** (€220k–€380k). Drive the outskirts urbanizaciones: **Monte Alto, Miravalle, La Charca, Sierra de Alpujata slopes**. Look for "se vende" / "for sale" signs. Coín pueblo casco is narrow — DON'T drive the van into the old centre. Park on the A-355 ring road.
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Monte+Alto+urbanizacion+Coin+Malaga

### Lunch — Coín outskirts
- **13:30** Lunch: **Venta San Francisco** on the A-355 toward Marbella (Coín outskirts). Large parking, family-style, indoor and terrace, traditional. Verify by phone before you go.
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Venta+San+Francisco+Coin+A-355+restaurante

### Afternoon — Alhaurín el Grande + back to base
- **15:00** Drive **Alhaurín el Grande** (20 min west of Coín). Drive the urbanizaciones:
  - **El Zangano, El Cigarral, Camino de la Pañoleta, Llano de la Mejorada** — these are the plot-heavy zones.
  - Stock up at **Mercadona Alhaurín el Grande** (Ctra. Cártama 25, on the way back to Alhaurín de la Torre).
    - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mercadona+Carretera+Cartama+25+Alhaurin+el+Grande
- **16:30** **Pinos de Alhaurín proper walk-around** — drive every street looking at "se vende" signs. Specifically the wider avenues (Av. las Palmeras, Calle Jarales, Calle Tomillar, Calle Almendros). These are 500–1,000 m² plot streets with active resale.
  - Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Avenida+las+Palmeras+Pinos+de+Alhaurin+Alhaurin+de+la+Torre
- **18:00** If you have appointments made: meet a local **inmobiliaria** in Pinos de Alhaurín for plot viewings. Recommended agencies: Engel & Völkers Alhaurín de la Torre, Right Casa Alhaurín. Pre-book by email the week before with your "wish list" (3–4 candidate plots) and they'll have keys ready.

### Evening
- **20:30** Dinner at base or a repeat of Venta El Ándalus. Rest early — Day 3 is the longest working day.

## 7. STRATEGIC OVERNIGHT BASES (villa rentals, 7-passenger-van friendly)

All bases share the same requirements: **wide internal streets (no tight turns), private driveway for the van, gated urbanization, 3-4 bed villa with private pool and AC (essential for August)**. Book via Airbnb, Vrbo, or a local Costa del Sol agency (Solaga, Solrural, Villaway) — local agencies often have the best urbanización stock.

### Night 1 & 2 — Inland Guadalhorce: **Pinos de Alhaurín, Alhaurín de la Torre**
- **Why:** wide avenues, family-residential, near 3 Mercadonas + Lidl Taralpe, 25 min to the coast jump, 15 min to Coín and Cártama.
- **Search filters:** "Pinos de Alhaurín" + "villa" + "private pool" + "parking".  
- **Price band Aug 2026 (high season):** €250–€400/night for a 3-4 bed villa with pool.
- **Caution:** inland nights run 26–28 °C; the AC must be able to cool the bedrooms all night for the child.
- Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pinos+de+Alhaurin+villa+rental+Alhaurin+de+la+Torre

### Night 3 — Coast: **Riviera del Sol / La Cala Hills, Mijas Costa**
- **Why:** central coast, equidistant to La Cala (north), Calahonda (south), Las Lagunas (east). Best position for Day 3 obra nueva + resale and easy westward Day 4 start.
- **Search filters:** "Riviera del Sol" or "La Cala Hills" + "villa" + "pool" + "parking".  
- **Price band Aug 2026:** €280–€450/night for a 3-4 bed villa with pool (coast > inland in Aug).
- Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Riviera+del+Sol+villa+rental+Mijas+Costa

**On-site verification is non-negotiable for any plot purchase.** Each parcel needs:
1. **Nota simple** (land registry excerpt, 24h, ~€10)
2. **Ficha urbanística** (municipal zoning certificate, 1–4 weeks, varies by municipality)
3. **Topographic + geotechnical study** (€1,500–€3,500)
4. **Architect feasibility study** (€2,000–€5,000)
5. **Confirm: building coefficient, max height, max occupancy, setbacks, protected-oak regulations** (very common in inland Andalucía)
6. Confirm utility connections: water (municipal or well), electricity (endesa Iberdrola connection fee), sewage (municipal or septic)

## 9. KEY QUESTIONS TO ASK AT EVERY STOP

### Obra nueva developer
1. Exact handover date for THIS unit (not the project headline date).
2. Is the price + IVA, and is parking + storage included? Any "community fees" extra?
3. Bank financing — which bank, what % LTV, what rate range? Get a pre-approval letter.
4. What guarantees does the developer give (Spanish law requires 10-year structural insurance, 3-year habitability, 1-year finishes — verify these are properly bonded, not just promised).
5. Is there a community rental pool program? What's the projected yield?
6. What's the IBI (property tax) annual estimate? Community fees per month?
7. Are there any pending PGOU changes in this sector that could affect views / build-out density?

### Terreno (plot) seller / agent
1. What's the classification — suelo urbano consolidado, no consolidado, or urbanizable sectorizado?
2. What's the build coefficient (m² buildable / m² plot)? Max occupancy, max height, max depth?
3. Are there oak-tree (encina / alcornoque) protection zones? Cultural / archaeological protections?
4. Is the build permit in place, or is it a developable sector that requires the seller to pay for the Plan Parcial first?
5. Connection fees: water, electric, sewage — and is the access road paved + a public road?
6. Is the nota simple clean? Any embargos, mortgages, or servidumbres?
7. For modular/industrialized builds: has the local PGOU been confirmed to accept Declaración de Obra Nueva for factory-built modules? (Per Ley LISTA 2022, it should, but each municipality interprets differently.)

### Resale (existing house / apartment)
1. Why is the seller moving? (Genuine vs. distress)
2. Last IBI receipt, last community-fee receipt (for apartments).
3. Energy performance certificate (CEE) — Spanish law requires seller to provide at viewings.
4. Habitation certificate (cédula de habitabilidad) in date.
5. Annual community fees and any pending derramas (special assessments).
6. Year of last major reform: electrics, plumbing, roof, AC.
7. Are there any known issues with humidity (very common in coastal apartments below ground floor).

## 10. CAVEATS — WHAT THIS PLAN DOESN'T DO

1. **Developers' specific stock in August 2026.** Phases sell out; specific unit numbers and availability shift week-to-week. Always call the sales office 48h before you visit to confirm the show-home is open in August (many close for 1–2 weeks in August — Taylor Wimpey and Metrovacesa are usually open, smaller builders may not be).
2. **Padel league schedules.** Social-mixto evenings are listed on Playtomic (https://playtomic.com) — book 2–3 days ahead, or you can drop in and ask at the front desk.
3. **Schools in August.** Most are closed 1–20 August. Use August for the **drive-by** to assess location, neighborhood, drop-off traffic. Book the actual tour for June/July or post-20 August.
4. **Villa rental availability for August 12–15 2026.** Book NOW. The week of 15 Aug is the peak of the high season for the Costa del Sol and books out by April. Use Airbnb's "Reserve instantly" filter to lock immediately, then message the host to confirm the driveway is wide enough for a 7-seater van.
5. **Restaurant rotation.** August on the Costa del Sol sees ~10% of restaurants close for 1–2 weeks on family holidays. The 3-day-advance phone call to your restaurant is non-negotiable for August.
6. **NABSS-accredited school list** changes annually — pull the 2026 list from https://nabss.org before booking any campus tour.
7. **Spanish tax / legal advice is not in scope.** For any terreno or significant purchase, engage a local **abogado** (lawyer) and **gestor** (tax administrator). Typical fees: 1% of purchase price for the lawyer, €500–€1,500 for the gestor, plus notary + registry (another 1–1.5% combined). Recommended: **Montero-Aramburu** or **Aranguren Abogados** in Málaga.
