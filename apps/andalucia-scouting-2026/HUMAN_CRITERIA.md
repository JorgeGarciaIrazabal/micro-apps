# Andalucía Retirement Scouting: Human Evaluation Criteria (HUMAN_CRITERIA.md)

This document explains the logical framework, weights, and rules used to evaluate properties and locations during the Andalucía real-estate scouting trip. It is designed to align your family goals with real-world logistics.

---

## 🎯 1. Core Objectives
*   **Retirement Target:** Find a quiet, safe, and family-friendly community to retire with your family (5 people, including 1 young child).
*   **Primary Location:** Suburban towns surrounding **Granada** (Las Gabias, Churriana de la Vega, Alhendín).
*   **Fallback / Benchmark Locations:** **Torre del Mar** (Málaga Coast) and **Bormujos** (Sevilla Aljarafe).
*   **Financial Cap:** Strict ceiling of **€380,000** for a 3-bedroom property, with a strong preference to find excellent deals for much less (e.g., €120k–€200k) to keep capital liquid.

---

## 📊 2. Evaluation Criteria & Weightings

### ⚖️ A. Financial Efficiency (Weight: 40%)
*   **Purchase Cost:** Direct cost of the property. Can we secure a 3-bed condo under €200k?
*   **Taxes & Buying Fees:** Budget an additional **11–12%** in Andalusia for resale tax (ITP), notary, and registration fees.
*   **Ongoing Maintenance:** Low monthly community fees (gastos de comunidad) and property taxes (IBI). Condos with minimal communal gardens and basic pools are preferred over luxury developments with high overhead.

### 🌡️ B. Climate & Comfort (Weight: 30%)
*   **Summer Heat:** How tolerable is the summer? Sevilla (Bormujos) is extremely hot (frequently 40°C+). Torre del Mar has mild coastal weather (30°C max). Granada sits at 750m elevation, offering hot days but cool, comfortable night breezes from the Sierra Nevada.
*   **Winter Cold:** Granada experiences actual winter (temperatures down to 2°C). Málaga remains warm. Condos must have built-in central heating (aerotermia or radiators) in Granada, whereas Sevilla/Málaga only require AC heat pumps.

### 🚗 C. Vehicle & Parking Logistics (Weight: 20%)
*   **Double Car Fit:** The retirement plan involves owning 1 or 2 vehicles. 
*   **Town-wide Parking:** Can you easily park the cars around the shops, parks, and restaurants? Modern suburbs (Las Gabias, Alhendín, Bormujos) are designed for cars and have wide streets. Older coastal towns or historical cores have narrow streets where finding space is difficult.
*   **Secure Garage:** Condos must include a designated underground parking space (plaza de garaje) in the price simple.

### 🚇 D. Proximity & City Access (Weight: 10%)
*   **City Proximity:** Access to world-class dining, culture, and hospitals (Granada city center or Seville center) in under 20 minutes.
*   **Park-and-Ride Access:** Proximity to a metro station with a large park-and-ride lot, allowing you to bypass city center parking challenges completely.

---

## 🗺️ 3. How to Score Each Stop on the Trip

Use the following questions to grade each neighborhood you drive through:

1.  **Street Width:** Are the streets wide enough to easily maneuver a large SUV/van? Or are they narrow, single-lane roads with tight corners?
2.  **Condo Density:** Are the buildings modern (built after 2000) with private underground parking? Or are they high-rise blocks with street-only parking?
3.  **Local Amenities:** Can you walk or easily drive to a Mercadona or Lidl in under 5 minutes?
4.  **Family Safety:** Are there green parks, flat stroller-friendly sidewalks, and low-traffic areas for the child?
