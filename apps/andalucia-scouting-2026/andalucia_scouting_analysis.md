## 2. Detailed Town Analyses (Condos & Parking Focus)

### 🏔️ Granada Vega (Primary Target)
Granada is the standout winner for your goals. It has a cooler mountain-influenced climate and incredibly cheap condo pricing, leaving you with €180k–€270k in savings from your €380k budget.

*   **Las Gabias (Granada):**
    *   *Why Visit:* Condos around the golf course or the *Híjar* expansion are incredibly cheap (€110k–€170k). 
    *   *Parking & Cars:* Very easy. Streets in modern developments are wide (10–12 meters) with parallel parking spaces. Almost all post-2005 condo buildings include secure underground garages.
*   **Churriana de la Vega (Granada):**
    *   *Why Visit:* Flat, walkable, and getting a direct Metro connection to Granada city. 
    *   *Parking & Cars:* Modern avenues (e.g., Avenida Cristóbal Colón) offer easy street parking and guest spaces. The historic core is tight—avoid driving the van there.
*   **Alhendín (Granada):**
    *   *Why Visit:* Immediate A-44 highway access for easy driving. Offers larger residential condos with terraces for €130k–€195k.
    *   *Parking & Cars:* Highly car-friendly. Modern complexes like *Los Llanos* have abundant free curbside parking and spacious underground garages.

### 🌊 Málaga Coast
*   **Torre del Mar (Málaga):**
    *   *Why Visit:* Coastal condo living at its best. It has a flat, 4 km beachfront promenade and a temperate sea breeze.
    *   *Parking & Cars:* Excellent parking in the new *Poniente* district, featuring large, open-air public parking lots near the beach. However, in July and August, central street parking is congested with tourists. Underground parking is a must-have for your condo purchase here.

### 💃 Sevilla Aljarafe
*   **Bormujos (Sevilla):**
    *   *Why Visit:* Modern suburban condo developments (€150k–€220k) with excellent services.
    *   *Parking & Cars:* Built for cars. Broad avenues, large shopping center lots, and a massive park-and-ride Metro station nearby (Ciudad Expo) where you can park for free and ride into central Seville, avoiding city parking stresses.
    *   *Climate Alert:* Extreme August heat (40°C+) means high summer electric bills for 24h AC.

## 3. Financial & Budget Planning

### **Estimated Monthly Living Cost (Family of 3, Excluding Rent/Mortgage)**

*   **Granada Vega: ~€1,200 – €1,450 / month**
    *   Utilities (Electricity, Heating, Water, Fiber): €150 (Granada winters require gas/electric heating; summer AC is moderate).
    *   Groceries: €450 (highly affordable local produce).
    *   Dining & Tapas: €350 (free tapas with every drink keeps restaurant bills very low).
    *   Local Property Taxes (IBI) & Condo Community Fees: €70 – €100.
    *   Private Health Insurance (Required for residency): €150 – €250 (family plan).

*   **Torre del Mar: ~€1,500 – €1,800 / month**
    *   Utilities: €140 (low heating, moderate summer AC).
    *   Groceries: €500.
    *   Dining & Leisure: €500 (coastal/tourist pricing).
    *   Condo Fees (often includes pool/gardens): €120 – €160.
    *   Private Health Insurance: €150 – €250.

*   **Bormujos (Sevilla): ~€1,400 – €1,650 / month**
    *   Utilities: €200 (high electricity cost in July/August due to constant AC).
    *   Groceries: €480.
    *   Dining & Leisure: €400.
    *   Condo Fees: €80 – €120.
    *   Private Health Insurance: €150 – €250.
